Your notes are private

All your notes are stored only on your device — they are never sent to any server or cloud service. This also means your notes stay with the device and browser you use. Use Export (the ⇄ icon on the main screen) to back up your notes to a file.

About the optional setup steps

The Siri Shortcuts, Google Assistant, and home screen (PWA) setup steps described below are all completely optional. Remember works perfectly well without any of this — just open the link in your browser and start writing. These features are extras for users who want them.

Installing Remember on your Home Screen

On iPhone or iPad (Safari): Open the Remember web address in Safari, tap the Share button (the box with an arrow pointing up), then tap Add to Home Screen and tap Add.

On Android (Chrome): Open the Remember web address in Chrome, tap the three-dot menu, then tap Add to Home Screen.

Opening Remember with Siri (iPhone & iPad)

You can open the Remember app using your voice with Siri on iPhone or iPad. You will need to do a one-time setup first — this takes about two minutes.

Step 1. Open the Shortcuts app on your device. This is an Apple app that comes pre-installed — look for an icon with a colorful square. If you can't find it, ask Siri: "Open Shortcuts".

Step 2. Tap the + (plus) icon in the top right corner to create a new shortcut.

Step 3. You will see a search box that says "Search Actions". Type Open URLs (plural — with an S at the end).

Step 4. Tap Open URLs in the list that appears below the search box.

Step 5. In the URL field that appears, paste or type this address:
https://lynn-edu-apps.github.io/everyday-apps/remember/
Then dismiss the keyboard (usually by tapping a checkmark ✓ or tapping anywhere outside the keyboard).

Step 6. At the top of the screen you will see the name of the shortcut. Tap on it and rename it to something easy to say, such as Open Remember.

Step 7. Tap Done to save.

From now on, just say "Hey Siri, Open Remember" and Siri will open the app for you.

Adding a new note with Siri (iPhone & iPad)

You can create a shortcut that lets you say "Hey Siri, Add a note to Remember" and Siri will ask you what to write, then open Remember with your words already in a new note.

Step 1. Open the Shortcuts app and tap + to create a new shortcut.

Step 2. Search for and add Ask for Input. Set the prompt to something like "What would you like to note?" and set the input type to Text.

Step 3. Tap + to add another action. Search for and add URL (not "Open URLs"). In the URL field, type:
https://lynn-edu-apps.github.io/everyday-apps/remember/?action=new&content=
The result of Step 2 will be automatically piped into this step — you do not need to add any variable manually. You should see a dashed connecting line between the steps showing they are linked.

Step 4. Tap + again and add Open URLs. It will automatically use the URL from Step 3 — no need to fill anything in.

Step 5. Name the shortcut Add a note to Remember and tap Done.

You may notice Apple automatically adds a step at the top that says something like "Receive Apps and 18 more from Nowhere". This is normal and harmless — you can safely ignore it.

Note: These instructions are for iOS 26 and later, which uses a pipeline model where steps are automatically connected by dashed lines. On older versions of iOS, the steps may look different — you may need to manually insert a "Provided Input" variable into the URL field in Step 3.

Now say "Hey Siri, Add a note to Remember" — Siri will ask what you want to write, and your words will appear in a new note ready to save.

Opening Remember with Google Assistant (Android)

On Android phones you can open Remember by voice using Google Assistant. There are two ways:

Simple way (no setup needed): Just say "OK Google, open lynn-edu-apps.github.io/everyday-apps/remember" and Google Assistant will open the app in Chrome.

Better way (one-time setup for a shorter phrase):

Step 1. Open the Google app on your phone (the colorful G icon).

Step 2. Tap your profile picture in the top right, then tap Settings.

Step 3. Tap Google Assistant, then tap Routines.

Step 4. Tap the + button to add a new routine.

Step 5. Under "When I say...", type Open Remember.

Step 6. Under the action, choose Open website and paste:
https://lynn-edu-apps.github.io/everyday-apps/remember/

Step 7. Save the routine. From now on say "OK Google, Open Remember".

Sharing text into Remember (iPhone, iPad & Android)

Remember can receive text shared from other apps — such as a web page, an email, or a message — and automatically create a new note with that content.

How to use it: In any app, select some text or tap the Share button, then look for Remember in the share sheet. Tapping it will open Remember and create a new note with the shared text.

Note: The share sheet option appears after you have installed Remember on your home screen and may require refreshing the share sheet list in your device settings.

Available URL actions (for Siri Shortcuts)

Remember supports the following URL actions for use in Siri Shortcuts or any automation tool. Replace the base URL with your deployed address.

Create a new note:
…/remember/?action=new
…/remember/?action=new&content=Your+text+here

Open an existing note:
…/remember/?action=open&name=Shopping+List
Opens the note whose name matches (case-insensitive, partial match allowed). If no name is given, opens the most recently edited note.

Open a note and speak it aloud:
…/remember/?action=speak&name=Shopping+List
Opens the note and attempts to read it aloud automatically. Note: some browsers require a tap before allowing audio — if speaking doesn't start automatically, tap the Speak button.
